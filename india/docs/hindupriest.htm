Indians Essential to the British Army

The British empire in India was administered locally by Indians from a wide variety of castes and communities. Aware of religious sensitivities, the British made some attempt to provide a Hindu diet for the army and made arrangements to acknowledge Hindu festivals. This image shows a Hindu priest garlanding the flags of the 35th Bengal Light Infantry at a 'Presentation of Colours' ceremony.
