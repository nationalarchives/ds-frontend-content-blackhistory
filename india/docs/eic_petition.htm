Raising Soldiers for India

In this 'memorial and petition' to the King, the Court of Directors of the East India Company, East India House, London, seeks a licence to increase their military forces in the East Indies. They request authorisation to enlist 2,000 soldiers in times of war and 1,000 in times of peace, for 'the security and protection of their trade and possessions'.

CO 77/26, ff. 104-5 (1789)
