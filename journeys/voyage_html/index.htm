An 18th Century Voyage of Discovery

What do the following have in common?

Goldney Hall in Bristol

A country house bought by Thomas Goldney II, a Bristol merchant, in 1705.

  
 

The Voyage of Woodes Rogers

Two ships leave Bristol in 1708 on a privateering voyage under the captaincy of Woodes Rogers.

Robinson Crusoe

Alexander Selkirk, the inspiration for Robinson Crusoe, was marooned on an island for four years and four months. He was rescued in 1709.

  
 

The Slave Trade

In 1708 goods from Britain were being traded for slaves in Africa. These slaves were taken and sold in the Caribbean and Americas.

Ironbridge, Shropshire

Completed in 1779, this bridge is a symbol of the Industrial Revolution
