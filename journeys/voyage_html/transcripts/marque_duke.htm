22 Aprill 1708
Appeared personally M[aste]r Jelfe, and on ye behalfe of Cap[tain] Rich[ar]d Parrot now at Bristoll produced a Warrant from his Roy[a]ll Highness Prince George of Denmarke etc Lord High Admiral of Great Britain Ireland etc and of all Her Ma[jes]ties Plantations etc for the granting of a Comm[issi]on or L[ett]ers of Marque to the said Richard Parrot and in pursuance of Her Ma[jes[ties Instructions to Privateers made the following Declaration viz. that the said Cap[tain] Parrot his ship is called the Jamaica Gally, and is of ye burthen of ab[ou]t one hundred & seaventy Tonns, that the said Rich[ar]d Parrot goeth Cap[tai]n of her, that she carryes Eight Guns, twenty five men, twenty five small Armes, twenty Cutlaces, Six barrells of powder, Twenty Rounds of great Shott, and ab[ou]t Five hund[re]d w[eigh]t of small shott, that the said Ship is victualled for Six months, has two Suites of Sailes, three Anchors, three Cables, and ab[ou]t two hund[re]d w[eigh]t of spare Cordage, that W[illia]m Parrot goes Lieuten[an]t , Stephen Williams Gunner, M[aste]r Jones Gunner, Benja[men] Bush Carpentar and Humphrey Williams Cooke of ye said Ship, And that W[illia[m] Swimmer Esq[uire] Henry Swimmer, Abraham Elton, & John
Elbridge of Bristoll Merch[ant]s, and he the Declarent are the Owners & Setters out of the said Ship.
John Jelfe
The same day This Declaration was made before Geo[rge] Bramston Jun[ior?]
